Dead Poets' Pen Pal

Answer 8 mood/life questions + one free-text line ('what do you secretly miss'). Discover which historical letter-writer (Keats, Virginia Woolf, Oscar Wilde, a Pony Express rider, a Cold War spy, a Renaissance alchemist, etc.) is your epistolary twin — and receive a personalized one-page letter addressed to you, written in their voice, referencing your answers, styled as a yellowed handwritten page.

Group Chat Energy Audit

Paste 5–10 messages you've sent in your group chat (or enter your name + pick 4–6 behavioral chips). The app produces a formal-looking OSHA-style inspection report with a verdict role ('Certified Chaos Engineer,' 'The Silent Auditor,' 'Designated Vibe Supervisor'), a bulleted list of citations quoting your own messages back, a hazard rating, and a stamp. Downloadable PNG with a 'get your own audit' CTA for recipients.

Petty Small Claims

Fill a mock small-claims-court intake (plaintiff, defendant, grievance, damages, one aggravating-factor chip) and receive an official 1950s-typewritten court judgment — computed damages with absurd micro-damage line items, three LLM-generated findings that quote your grievance verbatim, and one of eight flattering-to-the-plaintiff verdict archetypes. Shareable #fragment URL re-hydrates the exact judgment without burning a second LLM call.

Split the Bill With Spite

Enter 3-6 people's names and paste one line per person describing what they did at dinner (e.g. 'Ben ordered the lobster,' 'Sarah forgot her wallet again,' 'Kai talked about crypto for 40 minutes'). The app returns an itemized bill-split receipt with a spite-adjusted dollar amount per person plus a named verdict per person ('The Unpaid Narrator,' 'The Calendar Saboteur,' 'The Lobster Heiress'). Rendered as a modern Venmo/Splitwise-style receipt graphic with a punchy sans-serif display face. Deterministic on input; shareable via URL fragment that replays the same receipt.

Fortune Vending

A pixel-art vending machine dispenses today's fortune. Drop a coin, mash the big red button, watch a can clunk down the chute with a satisfying thud, then peel the tab to reveal one hand-stamped line — specific, dated, same for everyone today. Three cans max per day so the ritual stays scarce.

The Acquisitions Editor

Type one paragraph pitching your weirdest niche interest; a jaded mid-list publishing editor writes back a 4-line rejection-or-acquisition letter on your idea, with an absurd advance figure ($1.18 to $9,840) and a 'comp title' nobody's heard of.

Kite String Physics

A single-line kite you fly on touch/mouse drag — the cursor is the reel, the wind is a live vector field you can see as faint streamlines. Let out line, reel in, sweep across the sky. Procedural wind gusts push the kite in legible ways; a small bird flocks around you; a cloud occasionally drifts through. The only goal is staying aloft longer and doing loops. Each loop/figure-eight/nosedive-recovery earns a named 'trick' stamp ('double cobra', 'faint heart'). After ~60s a pocket 'flight log' card gets stamped with your route (silhouette of the actual path you flew) + the tricks you landed + day's wind label ('apr 19: gusty southerly'). Share = URL-fragment encoding of the seed + path, recipient sees your exact flight replay then takes their own.

Wiki Tunnel

Daily Wikipedia race: everyone gets the same start and end article. Click only links inside Wikipedia pages to get from one to the other. Your click count earns a named verdict (Synapse Sniper / Tunnel Visionary / Tangent Tourist / Lost Wanderer) based on how close to the optimal-known path you got. Share card encodes the day's seed and your route as a 4-icon trail.

Texting Decoder

Reply to six rapid-fire texts the way you actually would (hey wyd, did u eat, cancel plans without being rude…). The app analyzes punctuation, capitalization, emoji use, abbreviations, and reply length to deterministically assign a named texting archetype — 'The Lowercase Romantic', 'Captain Capslock', 'The Ellipsis Poet', 'The "lol" Linguist', etc. The result shows your actual replies quoted as iMessage-style bubbles with annotated tells, ready to screenshot. Seeded: same 6 replies always yield the same archetype. Pastel-zine aesthetic with Archivo Black display type.

Particle Life

A Particle Life simulation in the browser: hundreds of coloured particles follow simple attraction/repulsion rules that produce emergent flocking, clustering, and cell-like behaviour. Users can tweak the force matrix sliders in real time and watch the world reorganise. One 'Screenshot my universe' button exports a named snapshot (seeded from the current rule set) for sharing.

Gravity Doodle

A falling-sand sandbox where users invent their own elements. Six built-in materials (wall, sand, water, oil, fire, plant) interact via composable physics rules; tap 'invent element' to generate a new one via AI — name it + describe its vibe and the LLM returns a structured spec (kind, density, colors, reactions with existing elements) that joins the palette for the session. Paint, pour, watch cascading reactions.

Reaction Diffusion Studio

A Gray-Scott reaction diffusion simulator running on HTML Canvas. Click or drag to seed chemical patterns anywhere on the canvas — watch Turing-style spots, stripes, and labyrinthine organism-like textures emerge in real time. Four preset modes (spots, coral, fingerprints, maze) plus three tunable sliders (feed rate, kill rate, diffusion ratio). A 'Save my organism' button exports the canvas as a full-resolution shareable image. No win state — pure mesmerizing emergent biology.

Boids Flock Painter

A canvas where a flock of 200 birds (boids) follows classic separation/alignment/cohesion rules. The user places colored 'seed' attractors by tapping or clicking — the flock swoops toward each one and leaves a glowing paint trail behind it. Each session produces a unique procedural painting shaped entirely by where the user taps. A 'Save my painting' button exports the canvas as a full-resolution image. No win state — pure hypnotic emergent art.
